Has THIS ever happened to you?

So you've been a bit busy lately - Working hard, playing hard, casting a few too many spells or cackling just a bit too evilly atop your dark tower on a night just a bit too cold... All of a sudden a destined hero barges in like he owns the place and starts wreckin' shop...

All your arcane bits, all your bobs and thingamajiggs, and you're just out of mana...

"I can't DEAL with this... I'm EXHAUSTED!"

But of course you are! How could you not be? Let's face it my dear fellow, this is no age for any kind of aspiring spellcaster...

Gone are the days that I, you, and all happy necromancers round this mortal realm could frollick round the fields, plucking daisies, lillies and the defiled skeletons of the fallen from the grass...

And how about THIS?

You've got it aaaaaaaall ready. All the materials neatly organised and in place...

All the runes formed, all the bones collected and polished to perfection, all the words chanted fearfully through the pale dark, without a single typo or invocation of wrath from Moloch...

All you need is your late victim's soul and you're set for a whole host of fun!

"This will be a breeze..."

You're thinking as you put on your marsh-boots and grab your trusty shovel or spellbound thrall, getting ready for a night with the boys out on the burial grounds...

Little do you know...

There's no soul on that grave! One of those pesky exhumations moving the body from this site to that and from thence yonder still, and all you, an honest-to-Belial gravedigger are left with is a note from Baron "What's-his-face" or Earl "Butt-muck-cares" telling you "We're sorry for your inconvenience, but if you'd like, feel free to take it up to bureau 14-1-z under statute 176.II.8 (but only after you get a seal of approval from Viscount "I've-just-about-had-it" from bureau 63a.84-XVIII-98, mind)" ...

Or maybe your precious corpse was handed over to the local hospital or parrish for "the betterment of mankind", or "the banishing of daemons" or all that malarkey, and now you might as well stick a pitchfork up your tuchus and roll over 'cause you're just about done.
